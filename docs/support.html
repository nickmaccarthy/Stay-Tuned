Frequently Asked Questions

Why isn't the tuner detecting my instrument?

Make sure you've granted microphone permission to Stay Tuned in your device's Settings. Also, try playing your instrument closer to your device's microphone and ensure you're in a relatively quiet environment.

What's the difference between auto and manual mode?

In auto mode, Stay Tuned automatically detects which string you're playing and shows how in-tune it is. In manual mode, you tap on a specific tuning peg to tell the app which string you want to tune.

How do I change the reference pitch (A=440 Hz)?

Go to Settings (tap the gear icon) and select "Reference Pitch." You can adjust the reference frequency from 432 Hz to 444 Hz to match your ensemble or preference.

What tunings are available?

Stay Tuned includes Standard, Half Step Down, Whole Step Down, Drop D, DADGAD, and many open tunings (Open G, D, C, E, A, and B). Tap the tuning name at the top of the screen to browse all available tunings.

What does "chromatic mode" do?

Chromatic mode detects any note in the chromatic scale (all 12 notes), not just the notes of your selected tuning. This is useful for tuning other instruments or checking intonation anywhere on the fretboard.

How accurate is the tuner?

Stay Tuned uses a professional-grade YIN algorithm with sub-cent accuracy. The app considers a string "in tune" when it's within ¬±7 cents of the target pitch, which is within the threshold of human perception.

Does the app work offline?

Yes! Stay Tuned works completely offline. All audio processing happens on your device, and no internet connection is required.
